# CIE Diagnostic — Relationship Domain Scenarios
## For Review — Do Not Load to Supabase Yet

Format: Story-based. Respondent identifies with a character, sides with someone, or decides what happens next.

Scoring key (internal only, never shown to users):
- 4 = identifies the systemic issue, asks the deeper question, or builds a structure that prevents the problem recurring
- 3 = engages but addresses the surface or stops one step short
- 2 = a legitimate instinct that has not been thought all the way through
- 1 = avoidant, dismissive, or closes the conversation down

No two options in any scenario score the same.

## BASE SCENARIOS
Everyone gets these regardless of profile.

---

### R1 — After the Business Argument

Camille and Trey had a real disagreement about a business decision. It got heated. They both said things that were sharper than they needed to be. They resolved the business issue. They never resolved the moment. That night they were polite. The next morning was normal. But something was sitting just below the surface.

**What is actually happening here?**

**A.** They are being mature. Not every argument needs a full debrief. `[2]`
**B.** They resolved the issue but skipped the repair. Over time those unfinished moments stack up. `[4]`
**C.** One of them needs to be the one to bring it back up. `[3]`
**D.** This is how long-term couples work. The politeness is the repair. `[1]`

---

### R2 — When You Stop Choosing Each Other

Lisa and Anthony have a good business. They have a good family. What they have less of is each other. Not because anything is wrong. Just because there is always something more urgent. The last time they were genuinely present with each other — not talking about the business, not managing something — neither of them can quite remember.

**What does this tell you about Lisa and Anthony?**

**A.** They are in a season. Every couple building something real goes through this. `[2]`
**B.** They have let the business become the most important relationship in the house. `[4]`
**C.** They need to start scheduling time together the way they schedule everything else. `[3]`
**D.** Anthony should plan something. Lisa has been carrying a lot and she needs to feel considered. `[1]`

---

### R3 — The Employee Feeling

Dominique has a strong personality in the business. She sets direction, closes deals, drives decisions. Her husband Marcus is talented but quieter. Over time the dynamic at work has started to come home. Dominique does not notice. Marcus does not know how to name it. He just knows that lately he feels like he works for his wife.

**What is Marcus experiencing?**

**A.** A confidence issue. He needs to find his voice in the business before this gets worse. `[2]`
**B.** A dynamic that started in the business and has leaked into the relationship. `[4]`
**C.** A communication gap. He has not told Dominique how he feels. `[3]`
**D.** Something that will work itself out as the roles in the business mature and balance. `[1]`

---

### R4 — Do You Actually Know What They Believe

Naomi and Stephen have been together for four years and building a business for two of them. They talk about everything. Or they think they do. One evening a topic comes up — faith, values, how they see something important — and Naomi realizes she does not actually know where Stephen stands. Not really. She has been filling in the blanks herself.

**What should Naomi do?**

**A.** Ask him. Not to start a debate but because she realizes she has been assuming instead of knowing. `[4]`
**B.** Let it go. Four years in, these conversations create more friction than clarity. `[1]`
**C.** Wait and see if it comes up naturally over the next few weeks. `[2]`
**D.** Share where she stands first and see if he meets her there. `[3]`

---

### R5 — Intimacy Under Pressure

Ava and Jordan used to be the couple people noticed. Easy with each other, present, affectionate. Two years into the business something shifted. Not a fight. Nothing happened. The business just got louder and the closeness got quieter. Ava misses Jordan. Jordan thinks Ava seems fine.

**What is Jordan missing?**

**A.** That the business growing and the relationship shrinking is not a trade-off Ava agreed to. `[4]`
**B.** That Ava needs quality time and he should plan something intentional soon. `[3]`
**C.** That Ava has not said what she needs so he cannot be expected to know. `[2]`
**D.** Nothing serious. Relationships have seasons and this is a building season. `[1]`

### R6 — When the Business Decision Is Also a Values Decision
**Condition:** faith_influence = high

Grace and Michael have always led with faith. It is part of how they parent, how they handle conflict, and how they built the business from the start. A new partnership opportunity has come up that would significantly grow their revenue. It sits uncomfortably with some of their values. Michael sees it as a business decision. Grace sees it as something bigger than that.

**What should they do?**

**A.** Take the meeting. You can have values and still look at an opportunity. `[2]`
**B.** Decline without a full discussion. Grace's discomfort is enough of a signal. `[3]`
**C.** Have a real conversation about where their line is before they respond to any opportunity like this. `[4]`
**D.** Let Michael handle it. He is more comfortable separating business from personal values. `[1]`

---

### R7 — When the Brand Gets Bigger Than the Agreement
**Condition:** political_importance gap between partners OR public_visibility gap >= 2

Chris and Dana have always kept certain conversations off the table. It has worked for them. But the business is growing and they are becoming more public — speaking, social media, community involvement. The things they used to leave unspoken are starting to have visibility. Their silence on certain topics is being noticed by their audience and by each other.

**What does this couple need to address?**

**A.** A clear line between what stays private and what goes public. `[3]`
**B.** Nothing. Couples do not have to agree on everything and a business does not need a political identity. `[2]`
**C.** A real conversation about whether their differences can coexist inside a shared public brand as the business grows. `[4]`
**D.** One of them needs to soften their position. A shared brand needs a shared voice. `[1]`

---

### R8 — Building Two Things at the Same Time
**Condition:** time_together = under-1 OR 1-3

Jasmine and Kevin have been together for two years and running their business for one of them. They are figuring out the relationship and the business at the same time. Some days it feels like too much. On those days they are not sure if they are fighting about the business or the relationship or both.

**What is the most important thing for Jasmine and Kevin to understand?**

**A.** They are doing two of the hardest things at once. That deserves acknowledgment, not more pressure. `[3]`
**B.** They need more relationship time that has nothing to do with the business. `[2]`
**C.** When they fight it matters which one they are actually fighting about. Mixing them up makes both harder to solve. `[4]`
**D.** They may have moved too fast. Building a business before the relationship is fully solid is a real risk. `[1]`

## Notes for Miranda

- R4 is the beliefs and viewpoints scenario you flagged earlier. The 4 answer is about realizing you have been assuming rather than knowing — not about agreeing, but about actually having the conversation.
- R5 option C scoring 2 is intentional. "She hasn't said it so I can't know" is a real thought many partners have but it puts the entire burden on the person who is already feeling disconnected.
- R7 is triggered by a visibility gap OR a political values gap between partners, since both create the same public brand friction.
- R6 option B scoring 3 not 1 — honoring a partner's values discomfort is not wrong, it just skips building the shared framework that would help them navigate it next time too.
