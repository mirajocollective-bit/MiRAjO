# CIE Diagnostic — Finances Domain Scenarios
## For Review — Do Not Load to Supabase Yet

Format: Story-based. Each scenario puts the respondent in someone else's situation.
They identify with a character, side with someone, or decide what happens next.

Scoring key (internal only, never shown to users):
- 4 = identifies the systemic issue, asks the deeper question, or creates a structural fix
- 3 = engages but only addresses the surface or stops short of the real issue
- 2 = a legitimate instinct that has not been thought all the way through
- 1 = avoidant, dismissive, reactive, or closes the conversation down

No two options in any scenario score the same.
Each option reveals a different type of person, not just a different behavior.

## BASE SCENARIOS
Everyone gets these regardless of profile.

### F1 — The Good Month Nobody Planned For

Marcus and Denise had their best quarter yet. Forty thousand more than expected. Marcus immediately started pricing out a new truck for the business. Denise booked the vacation they had been putting off for two years. Neither told the other before they did it. When the statements came in they had their first real fight in months.

**What should they do now?**

**A.** Agree on a split going forward — a set percentage that goes back into the business and a set amount that is personal, every time there is surplus. `[4]`
**B.** Have a long conversation about priorities so they understand each other better before the next good month comes. `[3]`
**C.** Let this one go and make a rule for next time. They were both doing what felt natural. `[2]`
**D.** Keep personal spending separate going forward so money does not become a recurring argument. `[1]`

---

### F2 — The Secret He Was Trying to Fix First

James has been managing the books for their marketing company. The last quarter was rough — down nearly $18,000 from where they projected. He has not told his wife Keisha yet because he wants to have a plan before he brings her a problem. He is working on it. She finds the statements on his laptop while printing something and sits in silence for a long time before he gets home.

**What should Keisha say when James walks in?**

**A.** She focuses on the numbers first. She asks him to walk her through where they actually are and what the plan is. `[3]`
**B.** She tells him what she found and asks him to help her understand why he carried this alone. `[4]`
**C.** She brings it up but keeps it light. She does not want him to feel attacked when he was already trying to fix it. `[2]`
**D.** She waits for him to bring it up. He knows she uses that printer. He will say something. `[1]`

---

### F3 — Her Account, Their Business

Tanya and Devon run a catering company together. The business account has $9,000. Their personal account is nearly empty after a slow month. Devon transfers $2,500 to cover their mortgage before talking to Tanya about it. He figures it is their business and their house, so it is all the same money. Tanya sees the transfer notification and her stomach drops.

**What is Tanya's real concern?**

**A.** The amount. $2,500 is significant and he should have checked in first. `[3]`
**B.** The pattern. If he moved money without asking once, there is nothing stopping him from doing it again. `[4]`
**C.** Nothing serious. He solved a real problem and the mortgage is paid. She should let this one go. `[1]`
**D.** The fact that she found out from a notification and not from him. `[2]`

---

### F4 — Two Years of Sacrifice, Two Different Finish Lines

Simone and Ray have been running their consulting firm for two years without taking a real salary. They finally have enough to make a move. Ray wants to reinvest everything and scale. Simone wants to start paying themselves. She is tired of feeling like they are building something they cannot touch. They are both right. They both know it. That is the problem.

**What should Ray say to Simone?**

**A.** Tell me what taking a salary would actually mean for you right now. `[4]`
**B.** Look at these numbers. Reinvesting now is the only way we get to where we want to be. `[1]`
**C.** What if we agree on a small salary now and revisit the split every six months as the business grows. `[3]`
**D.** Let's bring someone else in to weigh in so it's not just us going back and forth on this. `[2]`

---

### F5 — When Stress Stops Feeling Like Stress

Adrienne and Marcus have not been fighting. They have not been laughing either. The business has been slow for three months and neither of them has said that out loud yet. The conversations are shorter. The evenings are quieter. Adrienne thinks Marcus is pulling away. Marcus thinks Adrienne is disappointed in him.

**What is the most dangerous thing about this situation?**

**A.** They are both building a story about what the other person is thinking and neither story is accurate. `[4]`
**B.** The business problems are not getting solved because they are too busy managing the distance. `[3]`
**C.** They have not had a real conversation in a while and the longer they wait the harder it gets. `[2]`
**D.** Adrienne is carrying something that is not hers to carry alone. `[1]`

---

### F6 — She Approved It. He Found Out Later.

Lisa and Tony have a clear division in their business — she runs marketing, he runs operations. Lisa approved a $3,500 ad campaign without checking with Tony. In her mind it was her area and her decision to make. He found out when the payment hit and felt blindsided. She thinks he is overreacting. He thinks she crossed a line.

**Tony is upset. What is the most useful thing Lisa could say?**

**A.** You are right. Anything over a certain amount should be a conversation first, regardless of whose lane it is. `[4]`
**B.** I understand why you feel blindsided but this was my call to make. `[1]`
**C.** I should have told you. What would you need from me going forward? `[3]`
**D.** Next time I will tell you first. But I still think it was the right spend. `[2]`

### F7 — Everything Is Riding on This
**Condition:** primary_income = yes

Brendan and Camille quit their jobs eight months ago to run their e-commerce business full time. This month is the thinnest it has been. Brendan wants to take a small business loan to get through the quarter. Camille wants to cut everything they can before they take on debt. They are scared and they are showing it differently.

**What does this couple need most right now?**

**A.** A decision. One of them needs to make the call and they both need to commit. `[2]`
**B.** A real number. Exactly how long can they last and at what point does the loan become the only move. `[4]`
**C.** To say out loud that they are both scared. The decisions will be clearer once they stop pretending otherwise. `[3]`
**D.** Someone else to weigh in. A mentor or advisor who has been through this before. `[1]`

---

### F8 — Whose Debt Is It Anyway
**Condition:** debt_level = significant OR overwhelming

Before they started the business Nadia came in with $40,000 in student loan debt. Her husband Darius came in debt free. The business is growing but Nadia's debt sits in the background of every financial conversation. Darius has never said it is her problem. But Nadia feels it every time the budget comes up.

**What is Nadia really waiting for?**

**A.** For the debt to be paid off so she can stop feeling like a liability in her own marriage. `[2]`
**B.** For Darius to say, without her asking, that they are in this together regardless of where the debt came from. `[4]`
**C.** For a real plan that makes the number feel manageable instead of like a cloud over everything. `[3]`
**D.** For permission to bring it up without feeling like she is making it his problem. `[1]`

---

### F9 — The Opportunity That Costs Everything in Reserve
**Condition:** savings_runway = none OR 1-3-months

Jade and Chris have three months of runway left. A wholesale deal just landed that could 4x their revenue but it requires $12,000 upfront they do not have sitting still. Jade wants to find a way to make it work. Chris wants to protect what little cushion they have.

**What should they do?**

**A.** Take the deal. The risk of staying small is greater than the risk of running out. `[1]`
**B.** Pass. You cannot build on an empty foundation and three months is not enough cushion to risk. `[2]`
**C.** Map out what yes looks like in 90 days and what no looks like in 90 days, then decide. `[3]`
**D.** Find out if the terms are flexible. A payment plan or partial upfront changes the entire equation. `[4]`

## Notes for Miranda

- Every answer now reveals a different type of person: the fixer, the connector, the avoider, the pragmatist.
- No two options score the same in any scenario.
- The 4 answer is always the one that goes deepest — identifies the real issue, asks the better question, or creates a structure that prevents the problem from recurring.
- The 1 answer closes the conversation, avoids the issue, or is purely reactive.
- F8 option D scoring 1 is intentional — "waiting for permission to bring it up" is itself the problem. The person who scores highest recognizes what Nadia actually needs, not just what she is tolerating.
- Notes field prompt for this domain: "Anything about your financial picture you want to add? This stays with you."
