Outsourcing Awareness: Rebuilding Trust in Your Own Intuition

Miranda Johnson September 9, 2025

We all start with something natural.

A feeling.

An intuition.

A sense that says go or stop. It is simple. It is honest. And it is ours.

Then we grow up and life gets louder. We go through pain, mistakes, loss, new experiences. What once felt clear now feels confusing. So we begin to question the basics of what we already knew.

Instead of slowing down and listening, we look for validation. We want confirmation. Reassurance. Proof that we are doing it right.

So we start reaching outside of ourselves.

We want the reading.

We carry the crystal.

We ask someone else what they see for our future.

We wait for a sign.

None of those things are bad. But they become a problem when they replace our own knowing.

Now your intuition is filtered through objects or another person. And the more you do that, the further you move away from you.

Your connection gets weaker because you stopped using it.

"The answers are in nature. They always have been."

Here is the truth people skip. The answers are in nature. They always have been.

The feeling that it is about to rain.

The moment your body tells you something is not right.

The sudden urge to leave.

The calm that says stay.

That is guidance. No one had to translate it for you. You just knew.

But when you constantly ask something or someone else to validate what you feel, you begin to doubt yourself. You second guess. You override. You disconnect.

And then you wonder why you feel lost.

You are not lost. You just outsourced your awareness.

If you want to rebuild trust with yourself, start noticing again.

Notice your breathing.

Notice tension in your body.

Notice your energy around certain people.

Notice when peace shows up.

You do not need another tool. You need to return to you.

Spiritual growth is not about finding more answers outside of yourself. It is about remembering you already have access to them.
