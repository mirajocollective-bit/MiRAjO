Nutrition Guide

Non-Negotiable 7
Mindful Nutrition
This is not a diet and not a fast. It is a daily ritual to make sure your body gets what it needs to function at its best. Focus on fuel, not restriction. You are doing physical and mental work every day — your nutrition must match that.
Medical Disclaimer
The nutrition guidance in this program is intended for general wellness support and does not constitute medical advice. Before making significant changes to your diet or beginning any new exercise program, please consult your physician or a qualified healthcare provider — especially if you have an existing health condition, are pregnant, or are taking medication. Listen to your body and always prioritize your individual health needs.

Eat for Fullness
Choose foods with protein and fiber to keep you satisfied, not just filled. Craving-driven eating depletes your energy and focus.
🌈
Color Your Plate
A variety of colors means a variety of nutrients. Think of it as feeding your body the full spectrum — not just one lane.
💧
Hydration First
Hydration is a core part of nutrition. Your brain, muscles, and recovery all depend on water. Start every meal with a glass.
🧘
Slow Down
Notice how food makes you feel afterward, not just in the moment. Eating slowly improves digestion and helps you recognize true fullness.

Fuel Guide
Meal Ideas
Simple, balanced options to keep your body fueled across the day. Adjust portions to your size and activity level.
Morning — Before or after your first cardio session
Protein Oats
Rolled oats + protein powder + banana + almond butter. Fiber, slow carbs, sustained energy.
Egg & Veggie Bowl
3 eggs scrambled with spinach, peppers, onion. High protein, iron, vitamins.
Green Smoothie
Spinach + frozen berries + banana + Greek yogurt + water. Fast, nutrient-dense.
Midday — Fuel your afternoon session
Power Salad
Mixed greens + grilled chicken + chickpeas + quinoa + olive oil dressing.
Rice & Protein Bowl
Brown rice + salmon or tofu + roasted veg + avocado slices.
Lentil Soup
Red lentils + tomatoes + cumin + spinach. Filling, high fiber, anti-inflammatory.
Evening — Recovery and repair before sleep
Baked Salmon & Greens
Salmon + steamed broccoli + sweet potato. Omega-3s for muscle recovery and sleep quality.
Stir-Fry
Lean protein + mixed vegetables + brown rice + low-sodium soy sauce. Fast, filling, balanced.
Turkey & Veg Wrap
Whole grain wrap + turkey + hummus + cucumber + tomato. Light protein before bed.
Snacks — Between sessions when needed
Apple + Nut Butter
Natural sugars + healthy fat. Sustains energy without crashing.
Greek Yogurt
High protein, probiotics. Add berries and seeds for extra nutrients.
Boiled Eggs + Veg
2 boiled eggs + carrot sticks + hummus. Portable, filling, protein-forward.

The Practice
Mindful Eating in 6 Steps
Nutrition is not only what you eat — it is how you eat. These habits will help your body absorb more and your mind stay connected to your fuel.
01
Sit Down to Eat
No screens. No rushing. Eating standing or distracted breaks the mind-body connection and leads to overeating.
02
Breathe Before You Begin
One deep breath before you eat signals your nervous system to shift into rest-and-digest mode. Your body absorbs more.
03
Chew Slowly
Digestion begins in the mouth. Slowing down reduces bloating, improves absorption, and helps you recognize fullness.
04
Stop at 80%
Fullness signals arrive 15–20 minutes after you are actually full. Eat to 80% and pause. Your body will catch up.
05
Notice How You Feel After
Energized or heavy? Clear or foggy? Track what certain foods do to your performance and mood. Adjust accordingly.
06
Plan Ahead
When time is short, prepare simple balanced meals you can grab quickly. Hunger leads to poor choices. Plan to stay on track.
