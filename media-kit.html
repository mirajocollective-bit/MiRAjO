Biography

Miranda Johnson has spent 20 years building businesses, raising five children, and learning the hard way that performance is not the same as progress. She has worked across logistics, real estate, and residential construction. She founded MiRAjO Collective to help entrepreneurs stop shrinking and start leading from who they actually are. She currently serves as COO of Tampa Business Club and Events Chair for the Tampa Bay Latin Chamber of Commerce (TBLC). She has been invited to speak on affordable housing and leadership by the St. Pete Housing Authority at SPC The Epicenter in Clearwater, FL.

"I do not come to rooms to impress. I come to be useful."

She is the author of two books: The Pain Principle, which explores how suffering becomes the sharpest teacher, and LeadLoud: 11 Commandments of the Next-Gen Leader, a framework for entrepreneurs who refuse to lead small. Her courses, 25 Days and 25 Nights and Couples in Entrepreneurship, help individuals and business partners reset, realign, and move forward together.

Miranda is based in St. Petersburg, FL. Her faith is not a footnote. Her slogan is "Authenticity is the new Blue Check."

Signature Talk Topics
01
The Pain Principle

How your hardest chapter is your most credible asset, and how to stop hiding it. For audiences who need permission to use what they have been through.

02
Authenticity is the New Blue Check

Why entrepreneurs who perform for approval are losing, and what leading from identity actually looks like in a world of curated everything.

03
Seedtime and Harvest

A framework for entrepreneurs in the messy middle. The season before the result, and how to stay faithful to the work when the harvest is not visible yet.

04
Couples in the Trenches

What happens when your business partner is also your life partner, and how to build something serious without losing each other in the process.

05
Tech Without Losing Yourself

How small business owners can use AI, automation, and modern tools to grow without turning into someone they do not recognize.

06
Affordable Housing and the Leaders Who Lead the Way

The effects of the affordable housing crisis are not abstract. They show up in families, businesses, and communities. This talk explores what it looks like when leaders stop waiting for policy and start building solutions.
