The Framework

Most performance problems are not strategy problems. They are wiring problems. People work, lead, relate, and build differently based on how they are wired. Miranda brings a proprietary behavioral framework called DrivAmor that gives teams a shared language for how they think, how they conflict, and how they collaborate. One half-day. Permanent shift in how your people see each other.

Drive Mode
How people execute and lead

Four distinct drive profiles that explain why some people sprint and others sustain, why some lead from the front and others build from behind. Not better or worse. Just different wiring.

Amor Code
How people connect and relate

Five relational types that explain communication breakdowns, conflict patterns, and why some teams feel like family and others feel like friction. The missing layer in most team development work.

20 Combined Profiles
No two people are the same

Drive and Amor intersect into 20 unique profiles, each with a name, a set of strengths, and a set of blind spots. Your team walks away knowing exactly how they are built and how to work with each other.

The Outcome
A shared language, not a report

Miranda does not hand people a PDF and leave. The debrief is facilitated, the insights are applied to real work, and the language stays. Teams reference it in meetings months after the engagement.
